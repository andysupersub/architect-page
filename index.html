A note for specifiers

Standard details don't define the limits of the system.

The spans, panel sizes, and details shown here represent our standard pre-engineered scope under NZS 3604:2011.

In practice, many projects extend well beyond these parameters. If your project sits outside the standard details, talk to us early. Chances are we've already worked through something similar.

Specific Engineering Design (SED)

  • Larger panel spans beyond standard tables
  • Complex geometries and irregular floor plans
  • Exposed structural elements and feature panels
  • Multi-storey applications
  • Higher wind or snow zone requirements
  • Increased load conditions

Common questions from architects

Design and specification FAQs.

No. Architects and designers are not expected to document panel layouts within their architectural drawings.

Once the architectural and structural documentation has been finalised, we prepare the shop drawings required for manufacturing and installation. Your consent documentation remains focused on design intent, while our shop drawings provide the information needed for fabrication and construction.

SuperPanel is compatible with both absorbent and non-absorbent cladding systems installed over a minimum 18mm drained cavity in accordance with E2/AS1.

This includes most commonly specified claddings — brick veneer, timber weatherboards, plaster systems, and metal cladding. The selected cladding system must have an appropriate BRANZ appraisal and be installed with the specified underlay and flashing requirements.

Windows are fixed in accordance with the joinery manufacturer's requirements, with our sill, head, and jamb details providing the SIP-specific interface.

As with any highly insulated building envelope, glazing ratios and window performance should be considered carefully during design to minimise condensation risk and avoid unnecessary thermal bridging.

Yes. Our standard system has been engineered to accommodate projects within the "Very High" ultimate limit state wind classification under NZS 3604:2011, covering AS/NZS 1170.2 wind zones NZ1–NZ4.

Projects outside these parameters can typically be addressed through project-specific engineering.

Electrical services: Standard panels incorporate pre-formed 38mm service chases during manufacture, including two horizontal runs and one full-height vertical chase within a typical 1200mm-wide panel. We recommend involving the electrical contractor during the shop drawing phase to coordinate service locations.

Plumbing services: Plumbing is generally accommodated within a dedicated internal services cavity created by battens fixed to the panel face. Direct perpendicular penetrations through panels are acceptable; however, plumbing should not run horizontally or vertically within the panel core.

Often, yes. The published span tables reflect our standard pre-engineered offering rather than the absolute capability of the system.

Projects involving increased loads, larger spans, unusual geometries, or demanding environmental conditions can frequently be achieved through Specific Engineering Design. If you're considering something outside the published guidance, we'd encourage an early conversation.
